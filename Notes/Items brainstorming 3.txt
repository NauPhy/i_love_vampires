Introduction: Gun vs sword and mundane weapons
Most playable characters in the game start with 1 gun and 1 sword. By themselves they do nothing. Each one has 3 weapon slots, one of which will contain a mundane weapon at start (depending on the character). 

When you level up, if you aren't full on weapons, some of your level up options are likely to be known or unknown mundane weapons. When you acquire a mundane weapon, you can choose to slot it in either your gun or your sword. It will take a different form depending on which it's slotted into. For example, Mundane1 will be a musket in a gun slot and a sword in a sword slot.

Once you've upgraded a mundane weapon a couple of times, whenever an upgrade option would appear as a level up reward, it's replaced by an offer to specialize (evolve) the weapon into a specific set, which will not greatly increase its power but will make it cooler and allow further upgrades. Once it is fully upgraded, it can undergo a standard evolution that massively increases its power.

There are a few reasons behind this gameplay mechanic
1. It's new and interesting
2. It allows you to get your build started before committing to a weapon
3. If you get a weapon specialization you don't like, you can get a different level up reward, which is greedy but can pay off.
4. It lessens the advantage of deciding what you want to build before you start the run
5. Similar weapons (i.e. fireball and poison bolt) feel justified in their similarity if they evolve out of the same weapon.

Additionally, two of your early level up rewards (at set levels, 5 and 10 maybe) will allow you to swap out your gun or sword for objectively better versions that give bonuses to their slots. For example, instead of [revolver- no bonuses] you could choose between [obsidian dragon- slot 1 - +10% damage, slot 2 - +10% attack speed, slot 3 - +10% crit chance] and 2 other options.

Players can choose their gun/sword replacements based on their early evolution/specializations or vice versa.
